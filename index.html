Research

I'm currently analyzing neural phase coherence in nonhuman primates to understand how different brain regions synchronize during texture-guided sensorimotor behavior. This work extends my earlier statistical analysis of coherence magnitudes by shifting into true time-series territory: instead of looking only at overall peak values, I track how coherence evolves across time, trials, and experimental conditions. The goal is to quantify how neural communication changes under control, scopolamine, and scopolamine plus nerve-block conditions, and whether specific frequency bands (e.g. theta) show distinct patterns. This involves extracting coherence trajectories, comparing them across texture plates and region pairs, and identifying condition-dependent disruptions in rhythmic coupling.

I use both MATLAB and Python to run nonparametric statistical tests on the coherence data, giving me a flexible way to compare conditions without making assumptions the signals don't support. I design the analysis so each contrast across texture plates, frequency bands, and region pairs is controlled and interpretable. I'm also starting to layer in simple neural decoding, using coherence features to see whether patterns in rhythmic activity can predict experimental state.
